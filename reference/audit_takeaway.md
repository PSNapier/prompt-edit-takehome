## Funnel Strategy

This is a **cold traffic, single-offer funnel**. The visitor arrives knowing nothing about PromptEdit, so the page needs to do all the trust-building and convincing from scratch. The job of the page is linear:

**Hook them with the tool quality → prove it's legit → make the subscription the obvious choice → de-risk with guarantee**

No wandering. No sprawling template library detours before they've even bought in.

---

## Recommended Layout (Top to Bottom)

| #   | Section                        | Purpose                                                                                                                                           |
| --- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Nav**                        | Logo only. No external links that leak traffic                                                                                                    |
| 2   | **Hero**                       | Power headline + 1 demo video showing real AI output quality. One CTA.                                                                            |
| 3   | **Hook Expansion**             | "One platform. Every major AI tool. No stacked subscriptions." — short punchy copy block                                                          |
| 4   | **Tool Logos Strip**           | The recognizable brand logos (Suno, Heygen, Kling, Veo, etc.) as social proof of legitimacy                                                       |
| 5   | **Plugin callout**             | Dedicated section: DaVinci Resolve + Adobe Premiere Pro integration — this is a major differentiator for cold traffic and needs its own spotlight |
| 6   | **Tool Category Demo Section** | Image, Video, Audio — keep 1 hero demo video per category max. Cut the per-feature sub-videos                                                     |
| 7   | **Pricing**                    | Subscription front and center. One-time pack below it, clearly labeled as "rather try it first?"                                                  |
| 8   | **Guarantee**                  | Immediately below pricing to kill hesitation right at the decision point                                                                          |
| 9   | **FAQ**                        | Trimmed to 4-5 questions that directly address cold traffic objections                                                                            |
| 10  | **Footer CTA**                 | Repeat the subscription CTA one final time                                                                                                        |

## CTA Strategy

| CTA                             | Text                                                 | Placement                                               |
| ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary (repeated)              | **"Start Creating Today"**                           | Hero, after plugin section, after demos, pricing button |
| Pricing anchor CTAs (scrollers) | **"See Plans"**                                      | Early in page for people who scroll-skip                |
| Secondary / downgrade           | **"Just want to try it? Start with a credit pack."** | Below the subscription pricing card                     |
| Guarantee CTA                   | **"Start Creating Today — Risk Free"**               | Below guarantee block                                   |

One CTA. One destination. Repeated with contextual framing as trust builds down the page.

---

## Pricing Section Fix

The current pricing is the biggest trust killer for cold traffic because it presents two options with confusing overlapping math. Recommended restructure:

**Lead with the subscription card:**

- Clear price: $39/month
- Value anchor: "Get $49 in AI credits every month" (that's 25% free)
- 3 bullet benefits max
- "Start Creating Today" button

**Below it, smaller, muted card:**

- "Not ready to commit? Grab a one-time credit pack for $98 and get $110 in credits."
- "Upgrade to a subscription anytime."

This anchors the subscription as the default and frames the credit pack as a lesser fallback — not a competing offer.
